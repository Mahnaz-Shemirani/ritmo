About RITMO

Dance is for everyone — especially you.

RITMO was founded on a simple belief: that dancing makes life better. We are a welcoming community for adults who want to move, connect, and grow — regardless of prior experience.

Based at Tibblehallarna in Täby, we welcome dancers from across the Stockholm area — Sollentuna, Vallentuna, Danderyd, Åkersberga, Österåker and beyond. Easy to reach by Roslagsbanan and bus.

  • 🎯
    Small Groups Max 12 students per class ensures personal coaching and real progress.
  • 🏆
    Expert Instructors Certified freelance professionals with competition and performance backgrounds.
  • 🌍
    Inclusive Community No partner? No experience? No problem. Come as you are.
  • 🎵
    Authentic Music We dance to real Latin tracks — no watered-down compilations.

Month 1–2: Build Awareness (Free)
• Create Instagram & TikTok @ritmostudio — post 3–4 short reels per week of class highlights, "try it" moments, and your teachers doing showcase moves. Reels with Latin music get pushed hard by the algorithm.
• Join Facebook groups: "Latinodans Stockholm", "Expats in Stockholm", "Things to do in Stockholm" — introduce RITMO with a free trial offer.
• Post on local Swedish community boards (Bostadsportalen forums, Familjeliv.se).
• Ask every trial student to leave a Google Business review.

Month 2–3: Partnerships
• Contact local gyms (SATS, Actic, Friskis & Svettis) for cross-promotion — offer their members a free trial.
• Partner with nearby restaurants and cafés for flyer placement (Latin-themed venues especially).
• Reach out to HR departments of large Stockholm companies — corporate wellness packages.
• List on Fynd.nu, Groupon Sweden, and Studentkortet (if you want to attract that demo).

Month 3–6: Paid & Events
• Run a targeted Meta ad campaign: 200–500 kr/week, targeting Stockholm adults 25–50 interested in "dancing", "Latin music", "fitness". Cost per trial signup will be 30–80 kr.
• Host a free open social dance evening (milonga-style) — invite trial students and current students. Film it for content.
• Organize a 6-week challenge with a small prize — drives retention and word-of-mouth.
• Seasonal campaigns: January "New Year, New Moves", Valentine's Day "Dance Together" couples deal, Summer launch party.
Your compensation structure: 300 kr base + 30% of student revenue per class

Example: A class with 10 students at 180 kr drop-in = 1,800 kr → 300 + 540 = 840 kr per class for the instructor. That's very competitive for freelance dance teaching in Sweden.

Where to find instructors:

1. Swedish Dance Organizations
• Svenska Danslärarförbundet (SDDL) – Sweden's official dance teacher association. Contact them to post a freelance opening.
• Riksidrottsförbundet (RF) – lists certified fitness and dance instructors.

2. Salsa & Bachata Communities
• SalsaStockholm.se and Stockholm's Latin dance Facebook community (search "Salsa Stockholm")
• Attend local social dance nights (milongas, salsa socials) and recruit there directly — teachers often teach at multiple studios as freelancers.
• Bachata-Sverige community on Facebook

3. Job/Freelance Platforms
• LinkedIn – search "salsalärare Stockholm", "danscoach", "bachata instructor"
• Upwork / Fiverr for Dance Fitness instructors specifically (Zumba/Latin fitness certified)
• Blocket Jobb or Indeed.se – post a part-time freelance listing
• Facebook Marketplace / Groups: "Dansare Stockholm", "Frilanslärare Sverige"

4. Fitness Instructor Networks (for Dance Fitness)
• Contact Zumba-certified instructors via zumba.com/instructor-finder
• SATS and Friskis instructors often freelance on the side — post in their communities
• Gymhero.se instructor directory

What to look for: At least 1–2 years of teaching adults, references from other studios, and willingness to trial 2–3 classes before committing. Offer a simple freelance contract covering class coverage, cancellation, and revenue share timing.
Suggested Business Name: RITMO
("Ritmo" = Spanish/Italian for rhythm)

Why it works:
• Short, memorable, punchy — easy to say in Swedish and any language
• Instantly evokes music and dance without being literal
• Works for all three offerings: Salsa, Bachata, Dance Fitness
• Domain ritmostudio.se is likely available
• Great on social: @ritmostudio

Alternatives if taken:
• Compás (Spanish for beat/timing)
• Sabor Studio (sabor = flavor/soul in Latin dance)
• Movida Dance (movida = movement/scene)
• Clave Studio (clave = the fundamental rhythm pattern in salsa)

Register as F-skatt (sole trader) or AB: For starting out, register as an enskild firma at Bolagsverket.se. Simple, low cost. Once monthly revenue exceeds ~50,000 kr consistently, consider converting to AB for liability protection.
Conservative first-year scenario (8 classes/week, avg 8 students @ 180 kr):

Weekly student revenue: 8 × 8 × 180 = 11,520 kr/week
Monthly student revenue: ~46,000 kr

Monthly instructor costs (8 classes × 4 weeks):
• Base: 32 × 300 = 9,600 kr
• 30% share: 46,000 × 0.30 = 13,800 kr
• Total instructor costs: ~23,400 kr

Gross profit before rent/admin: ~22,600 kr/month

Growth scenario (12 students avg):
Student revenue: ~69,000 kr/month
Instructor costs: ~30,000 kr/month
Gross profit: ~39,000 kr/month

Key costs to budget:
• Studio rental (sublease evenings from a gym): 8,000–20,000 kr/month
• Music licensing (STIM): ~2,000–3,000 kr/year
• Insurance (Folksam freelance business): ~500 kr/month
• Marketing (Meta ads): 1,000–2,500 kr/month
